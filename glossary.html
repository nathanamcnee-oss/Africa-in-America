Glossary

For the casual reader, many of the terms sprinkled betwixt these pages will be unfamiliar. Rather than laboriously redefining them with each reusage, I've provided a glossary with short definitions of each word. I've also added short entries on certain scholars. A
Africanisms
Academic terms
Africanisms are cultural features that can be traced to African origins or African diasporic transmission (for example in language, music, religion, folklore, foodways, or burial customs).
B
Bakongo
KikongoPeople
A Kikongo-speaking people of West-Central Africa (the Kongo region).
bisimbi / basimbi
KikongoReligious terminology
Plural forms of simbi. The different plural prefixes can reflect different grammatical classes (for example, treating them as persons versus spirits).
Brown, Ras Michael
Person
A historian whose primary area of study is the religious and cultural development of the African diaspora. Associate Professor at Georgia State University. I use his work extensively on this site.
Bunzi / Mpulu Bunzi / Mangundazi
KikongoReligious terminology
A powerful simbi associated with rain, flooding, and water-driven change in the landscape; in some traditions, Bunzi is linked to spiritually potent stones.
C
Cabinda
Places
A coastal region in West-Central Africa, north of the Congo River mouth, historically tied to Atlantic trade routes and Kongo-world cultural exchange.
ciniumba
KikongoReligious terminology
A Kikongo term often glossed as “ghost.”
creolization
Academic terms
The formation of new cultural and linguistic forms under conditions of contact and coercion in the Atlantic world (especially slavery).
D
dikenga dia Kôngo
KikongoReligious terminology
A Kongo cosmogram (often drawn as a cross) used to represent life stages, seasons, elements, and the boundary between the land of the living and the land of the dead.
F
finda
KikongoReligious terminology
A Kikongo-derived word for “forest” (often written mfinda). It can refer both to a physical forest and to a spiritually charged space associated with danger, power, and liminality.
Fetiche Rock (Ntadi Nkisi / Taddi Moenga)
Religious terminologyPlaces
A named river landmark in West-Central Africa associated in local tradition with spiritual power and water-spirits such as simbi.
G
Gullah
Lowcountry
A coastal Lowcountry people and cultural tradition, especially associated with the Sea Islands. The term is also used for the Gullah language, a creole that preserves many Africanisms.
K
Kalûnga
KikongoReligious terminology
A great water boundary that divides the two sides of the mountain in the Bakongo cosmogram (dikenga dia Kôngo). It is often linked to the ocean or other waterways.
Kikongo
Kikongo
A Bantu language spoken by Kongo peoples in West-Central Africa. Many Atlantic-world terms — such as Kalûnga, simbi, and mpemba — are derived from this language.
kintumba
KikongoReligious terminology
The name for a religious specialist in certain Kongo-region traditions.
L
Lemba
KikongoReligious terminology
An initiation society found in parts of the Kongo cultural world.
loa
Religious terminology
A category of spirits in Haitian Vodou.
Lowcountry
LowcountryPlaces
The coastal plain of South Carolina and Georgia (and adjoining sea islands), historically defined by marshes, tidal rivers, plantation agriculture, and Atlantic trade connections.
luvèmba
KikongoReligious terminology
A stage of the dikenga dia Kôngo cycle, associated with the realm of death.
M
Mami Wata
Religious terminology
A widely circulating African-Atlantic complex of water-spirit images and stories (often represented as mermaid-like). It can function as a broad label for water spirits without replacing local categories such as simbi.
Manianga
Places
A region name in the Kongo cultural world.
Mayombe
Places
A forested region in West-Central Africa.
mermaid / sirène / sereia
Religious terminology
A European label (including sirène and sereia) used for water spirits. In African-Atlantic settings, “mermaid” can operate as a generic name for beings understood to inhabit and govern water.
Middle Passage
Academic terms
The forced Atlantic crossing of captive Africans to the Americas during the transatlantic slave trade.
minkisi
KikongoReligious terminology
Plural of nkisi: consecrated objects or compositions associated with spiritual power.
mpemba
KikongoReligious terminology
A Kongo term associated with “whiteness,” often linked to white clay and to the realm of the dead or spiritual transition.
musoni
KikongoReligious terminology
A stage of the dikenga dia Kôngo cycle, associated with the realm of the dead.
N
ndimba
KikongoReligious terminology
A term used in some Kongo traditions for a valley, stream, or water-adjacent setting associated with staged descent and transformation.
nganga
KikongoReligious terminology
A ritual specialist or expert (often glossed as healer, priest, or doctor) in Kongo traditions. A nganga may work with nkisi and oversee initiation and healing.
ngudi nganga
KikongoReligious terminology
A senior or lead expert role in some traditions.
nganga nkisi
KikongoReligious terminology
A specialist expert associated with nkisi practice.
ngolo
KikongoReligious terminology
A term used for inner energy, force, or power in some Kongo traditions.
nkisi
KikongoReligious terminology
A key Kongo term that can refer to a spirit and/or an object consecrated to that spirit.
Nkimba
KikongoReligious terminology
An initiation society (associated in some accounts with forest and water settings) that uses staged ordeals and resurrection symbolism to mark transformation.
nkulu
KikongoReligious terminology
A term for spirits of the dead or ancestral dead in Kongo traditions.
Nzo a Nkisi (“House of the Spirit”)
KikongoReligious terminology
“House of the nkisi”: a shrine or sacred place associated with nkisi practice.
Nzadi River
Places
A Kongo name associated with the Congo River (and sometimes with the idea of a great river boundary).
P
polyculturalism
Academic terms
An academic term describing a social world where multiple cultural traditions coexist and interact over time without necessarily blending. It is often used to emphasize overlapping “arenas” of practice before later convergence through a process known as creolization.
praise house
LowcountryReligious terminology
A small worship house central to African American religious life in parts of the Lowcountry.
S
seeking
LowcountryReligious terminology
A process of initiation in Lowcountry Christianity in which a candidate withdraws to the wilderness, fasts, prays, and has visions, before re-entering the community for examination and baptism.
simbi
KikongoLowcountryReligious terminology
Kongo nature spirits associated with water and other striking natural features. They are often described as guardians and intermediaries between the living and the dead; in the Lowcountry they can appear under related forms such as “cymbee,” “white babies,” or mermaid language.
Soyo
Places
A historical polity/region within the Kongo world.
W
white bundle
LowcountryReligious terminology
A recurring object in some Lowcountry “seeking” narratives: a mysterious bundle associated with spiritual power and transformation, sometimes discussed in relation to nkisi.
white baby
LowcountryReligious terminology
A recurring figure in some Lowcountry “seeking” narratives: a white or shining being encountered in visions, often connected to water and transformation.
kala
KikongoReligious terminology
A stage of the dikenga dia Kôngo cycle, associated with birth or dawn.
takula
KikongoReligious terminology
A stage in the dikenga dia Kôngo cycle, associated with maturity.
land of the living / land of the dead
KikongoReligious terminology
A Kongo and diaspora distinction between two mirrored halves of reality, separated by the Kalûnga. Important in the dikenga dia Kôngo.
